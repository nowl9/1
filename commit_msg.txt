fix(replay): position the replay clock from the first frame, not wall-clock

run() anchored the replay-mode SimulatedClock at datetime.now() -- build
step 1's placeholder for the reader-pending era.  With the ReplayReader now
driving the clock, that wall-clock anchor sits AFTER the recorded frames
(which predate "now"), so the reader's monotonic guard never advanced the
clock onto the recorded timeline: it stayed frozen at wall-time and every
recorded PM tick looked hours-stale.  The full --mode replay CLI path then
produced zero fills (only rejections) even though the reader-driven smoke
(which anchors before the frames) reproduced the chain.

- main.py: build the replay clock UNANCHORED (SimulatedClock("replay")); the
  reader positions it from the first frame's ts.
- replay.py: advance_to the first frame unconditionally when the clock is
  still unpositioned (clock.now() raises until positioned), then forward-only.
- test_replay.py: regression -- a reader with an unanchored clock (exactly as
  run() builds it) tracks the recorded timeline instead of freezing.

Verified end-to-end: `--mode replay --replay-date 2026-05-30` now reproduces
2 orders / 2 intents / 2 fills / 2 settlements (1 win, 1 loss) with no network.
Full suite: 734 passed.
