test(signals): pin filter-test clock -- kill the test_fresh_data_passes flake class

Root cause (confirmed empirically at the exact boundary): test_filters.py
captured `_NOW = datetime.now()` at pytest COLLECTION time (session t=0),
while SignalFilter._reject_stale_data compares fixture timestamps to the
wall clock at EXECUTION time (FilterConfig.max_data_age_seconds = 300 s).
test_fresh_data_passes builds a 30 s-old fixture and runs ~84-88% of the
way through the suite, so it fails iff suite-elapsed-at-execution > 270 s:
the two observed failures were the ~315 s full runs; ~275-285 s runs and
isolation (0.9 s) always passed.  A drift probe flipped the assertion at
exactly 270/271 s with the predicted reason ("options_data_age 301s >
max 300.0s").  State-leakage alternatives (settings singleton, structlog,
class caches, tracker singletons) were investigated and ruled out --
SignalFilter reads none of them.

Fix (test-side only; no production logic touched):
* tests/test_signals/test_filters.py: _NOW is now a PINNED literal
  (deliberately in the past -- any wall-clock comparison that sneaks back
  in fails loudly, not flakily) and a module-wide autouse fixture routes
  the production clock seam (filter()/explains() clock=, filters._ctx_now)
  through it.  This de-arms the same bomb in the ~19 sibling PASS tests
  (0 s fixtures, 300 s fuse) and the reason-flip case in
  test_stale_pm_data_rejected, and covers future tests in the module.
  Two new tests pin the seam's wall-clock fallback branch and the
  fixture's explicit-clock escape hatch.
* tests/test_execution/test_integration.py: the three SignalFilter call
  sites inject clock=lambda: _NOW (same 300 s-class exposure on the two
  PASS tests); _NOW itself stays wall-clock because the vol-surface tests
  there flow through seam-less datetime.now() code and need _EXPIRY in
  the real future.

Known same-class siblings left as-is (documented, margins not practically
reachable): 7 tests in tests/test_pricing/test_realized_vol.py couple to
RealizedVolTracker's 1 h query-time eviction window with ~87-118 min of
suite-runtime slack; test_integration's vol-surface tests flip only when
_EXPIRY (+14 d) passes.

Verification: target files green (44 + 28); three sequential full-suite
runs green: 874/876/876 passed -- the third took 322.45 s, SLOWER than
the ~315 s runs that flaked before the fix, exercising the exact failure
condition.
