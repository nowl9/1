docs(diag): Signal-seam diagnosis -- hard gate fired, passthrough not implemented

Phase 1 findings for the "Signal seam as single-signal passthrough"
goal. The diagnosis located the options-implied probability producer
and traced every handoff to the contract-matching/edge step, then hit
both stop conditions of the phase's hard gate, so Phases 2 and 3 were
not started and no source code was changed.

Producer: DigitalPricer.price_from_surface (pricing/digital_pricer.py:101)
already returns DigitalPrice, a frozen dataclass carrying the
(bid, mid, ask) probability triple plus method. The single handoff
point is main.py:412-423 (update_cache_from_surface -> cache.update),
and every downstream hop is already typed: CacheEntry ->
MatchResult.options_entry -> EdgeResult -> ArbitrageSignal/
ProbabilityQuote. A new frozen Signal dataclass would duplicate an
existing typed carrier, not introduce a missing seam.

Confidence is not produced at that boundary at all: it is computed
post-filter (main.py:502) by ConfidenceScorer.score from edge-step
outputs (edge_persistence, match_quality, book depth, options spread,
SVI fit RMSE) and backfilled onto passing ArbitrageSignals only.
Probability (per strike/expiry grid point, pre-match) and confidence
(per passing signal, post-filter) never co-exist at a producer
boundary, so a Signal{estimated_prob, confidence} passthrough cannot
be wired without moving confidence upstream (semantic change),
stamping a placeholder (already modelled by ArbitrageSignal's 0.5
default), or collapsing the bid/mid/ask triple into one scalar (lossy
for the conservative-edge math). All three are refactors, which the
gate forbids.

The replay path was verified to reuse the identical seams
(feeds/replay.py:372-374); no parallel pipeline recomputes either
value. Full field inventory at the boundary and the typed-carrier
chain are recorded in docs/diag_signalseam.md.
