feat(main): C5 keep-awake -- SetThreadExecutionState held for the life of --record-feeds

keepawake.py (new): keep_system_awake() context manager around
ctypes.windll.kernel32.SetThreadExecutionState.
* arms ES_CONTINUOUS | ES_SYSTEM_REQUIRED on entry, releases with
  ES_CONTINUOUS on ANY exit path (normal return, exception, Ctrl-C);
* per-thread state, so main() holds it on the main thread around
  asyncio.run for live --record-feeds runs only (replay/plain runs
  unaffected);
* display sleep deliberately still allowed (no ES_DISPLAY_REQUIRED);
* all failures logged + contained: keep-awake is a convenience, never
  a reason to refuse or kill a capture run; off-Windows it is a no-op.

Context: the 2026-06-10 stop was ENOSPC, not sleep (event logs
exonerated the OS), but sleep is the next-most-likely silent killer
for overnight captures on a workstation.

Tests: 5 new (arm/release order, disabled inert, release on body
exception, API-failure unarmed + no release, raising setter contained).
Full suite: 873 passed, 0 failed.
